STRICT RULES — follow every one:
1. LENGTH: Write EXACTLY 70 to 80 words. Count your words. If your draft is shorter, expand with additional facts from the articles. If longer, trim. This is the most important rule.
2. FACTS ONLY: Use only facts present in the snippets. Do not invent names, numbers, quotes, or details that are not there.
3. NO OPINION: No words like "shocking", "slams", "blasts", "historic", "remarkable". Just plain facts.
4. NO ATTRIBUTION: Do not write "according to [source]" or mention news outlet names.
5. FORMAT: Return ONLY the summary text. No headers, no bullet points, no preamble, no quotation marks around it.
6. TENSE: Use past tense for completed events, present tense for ongoing situations.
